Next steps

If you make your own arrangement you could agree to:

  • regular payments of a different amount
  • payments in kind for things like uniforms, haircuts and activities
  • share care
  • a combination of contributions

You could also agree to review your arrangement every year, when children reach milestones like birthdays or a change in schools, or if the income of either parent changes significantly.

Some parents find it useful to write down their arrangement (opens in new window or tab) so they have something to refer back to.

How and when to make payments

If you agree to a financial arrangement there are several ways to pay child maintenance. The most suitable will depend on your circumstances.

If you have agreed on fixed regular payments, you could set up a standing order. This is where money is sent directly from one person’s bank or building society account to another person’s account.

A standing order can help both parents to plan and manage their finances, and keep a record of payments made.

If you have agreed to more flexible payments, these could be made by bank transfer, cheque, or pre-paid credit card.

If you need help making an arrangement

If you cannot make an arrangement, or your arrangement stops working, you can:

  • ask a professional family mediator (opens in a new window or tab), friend or family member to help you
  • speak to Child Maintenance Options for support or more information

If both parents still cannot agree, or something else is stopping you making your own arrangement, the Child Maintenance Service may be able to help you.
