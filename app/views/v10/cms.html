How the Child Maintenance Service can help

The Child Maintenance Service may be able to help if you and the other parent cannot agree to a child maintenance arrangement.

Arrangements made through the Child Maintenance Service are not flexible, and cannot be adjusted to cover other costs or include payments in kind.

Use the online calculator to find out how much the Child Maintenance Service is likely to calculate for you.

You will have to pay a one-off application fee of £20, unless you are:

  • under 19
  • a victim of domestic abuse
  • living in Northern Ireland

Payment of the fee does not guarantee child maintenance payments will be made.

Making payments

Direct Pay

The Child Maintenance Service can work out how much child maintenance should be paid. You can then set up payments between yourselves. This is called a ‘Direct Pay’ arrangement. The Child Maintenance Service may recalculate the payment amount if your circumstances change.

Collect and Pay

If a Direct Pay arrangement does not work, the Child Maintenance Service may be able to collect the amount from the paying parent and pass it to the receiving parent – this is known as a 'Collect and Pay' arrangement. If this happens, you'll need to pay:

  • a 20% fee on top of your regular child maintenance payment if you're the paying parent
  • a 4% fee from your regular child maintenance payment if you're the receiving parent
Applying to the Child Maintenance Service

You can apply if you’re:

  • the parent the child lives with
  • the parent the child does not live with
  • a grandparent or other guardian of the child
  • a child over 12 living in Scotland

You will need to give information, including:

  • details about the child you are applying for – including the full names of both their parents
  • your National Insurance number
  • your bank account details, if the Child Maintenance Service needs to collect and pass on payments
