Arrange child maintenance

Child maintenance is support to help pay for your children's everyday living costs when you are separated from the other parent.

Studies show that children get the best outcomes when their parents are able to work together – this includes working together to arrange child maintenance.

Making an arrangement between yourselves might not be appropriate if you think the other parent poses a threat to you or your children, or if you are a victim of domestic abuse.

Both parents are legally responsible for the costs of raising their children, even if they don’t see them.

A child is someone who is either:

  • under 16
  • under 20 and in full-time training or education up to A level or equivalent

Arrange child maintenance yourselves

You can arrange child maintenance yourselves, if you can agree.

Your arrangement can include whatever works for both parents.

For example, you could agree to:

  • share care of the children
  • split the costs of specific items

Or you could agree that one parent will pay:

  • some of their income to the parent the children live with
  • for things like housing costs, uniforms, clothes, school trips, or after school clubs
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the children’s lives

No one else is involved, and the arrangement can be flexible to your circumstances.

Use the child maintenance calculator to see what the Child Maintenance Service would calculate for you.

Equally shared care of children

If the day-to-day care of children is shared equally between parents, one parent is usually not expected to pay child maintenance to the other. The cost of raising the children is covered by both parents.

In some cases, the Child Maintenance Service will calculate a ‘Nil rate’ of maintenance to be paid, for example if the parent who does not live with the child is a student, or is in prison. There are other situations when child maintenance payments can stop.

If your private arrangement stops working

You could ask a friend, family member or an independent family mediator to help you.

Some parents find this discussion guide helps them to get the conversation started and reach a new agreement.

Use the Child Maintenance Service

If you cannot agree child maintenance yourselves, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service for help.

The Child Maintenance Service can work out how much child maintenance should be paid and tell both parents.

There is a £20 fee for applying to the Child Maintenance Service, unless you are:

  • under 19
  • a victim of domestic abuse
  • living in Northern Ireland

The fee does not guarantee child maintenance payments will be made by the other parent.

There are no charges if you work with the other parent to set up your own child maintenance arrangement.
