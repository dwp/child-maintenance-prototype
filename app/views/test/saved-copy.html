Your child maintenance
calculation is
£40 per week or £173 per month

For an arrangement between John and Jayne, for Maisie, the Child Maintenance Service would calculate an amount of £40 per week or £173 per month.

There are many ways to arrange child maintenance between yourselves. You can use this calculation as a starting point for making your own arrangement.

The calculation is based on the following information provided 6 February 2019:



Questions Answers
How many children are you receiving child maintenance for? 1
How often do the children stay overnight with the paying parent? 1 to 2 nights a week
Does the paying parent receive any government benefits or State Pension? No
What is the total income of the paying parent? £433.89
Is this amount weekly, monthly or yearly? Weekly
How many other children live in the household of the parent paying child maintenance? 1

Next steps

You can now agree how you will share the costs of raising your children. Use this calculation as a starting point.

Your arrangement can include whatever works for both parents.

for example you could agree to:

  • share care of the children
  • split the cost of specific items

Or you could agree that one parent will pay:

  • some of their income to the parent the children live with
  • for things like housing costs, uniform, clothes, school trips or after school clubs

No one else is involved, and the arrangement can be flexible to your circumstances.
