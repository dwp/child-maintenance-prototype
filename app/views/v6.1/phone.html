Get help making or receiving child maintenance payments

If you cannot agree payments yourselves, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service for help.

You will have to pay an application fee of £20, unless you are:

  • under 19
  • a victim of domestic abuse
  • living in Northern Ireland

The fee does not guarantee child maintenance payments will be made.

Direct Pay

The Child Maintenance Service can work out how much child maintenance should be paid and help you to set up a payment arrangement between yourselves. This is called a ‘Direct Pay’ arrangement. The Child Maintenance Service will recalculate the payment amount if your circumstances change.

Collect and Pay

If a Direct Pay arrangement does not work, the Child Maintenance Service may be able to collect the amount from the paying parent and pass it to the receiving parent – this is known as a 'Collect and Pay' arrangement. If this happens, you'll need to pay:

  • a 20% fee on top of your regular child maintenance payment if you're the paying parent
  • a 4% fee taken off your regular child maintenance payment if you're the receiving parent.

Before you apply to the Child Maintenance Service

Before you can apply to the Child Maintenance Service you need to speak to Child Maintenance Options. They’ll talk with you about arrangement options. If you decide to use the Child Maintenance Service, they’ll give you a reference number and explain how to apply.

If you apply to the Child Maintenance Service

You can apply if you’re:

  • the parent the child lives with
  • the parent the child does not live with
  • a grandparent or other guardian of the child
  • a child over 12 living in Scotland

You will need to give information about you and your family, for example:

  • details about the child you are applying for - including the full names of both their parents
  • your National Insurance number
  • your bank account details, if the Child Maintenance Service needs to collect and pass on payments
