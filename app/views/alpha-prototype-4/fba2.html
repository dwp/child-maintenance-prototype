Arranging child maintenence yourself

You can arrange child maintenance between yourselves, if you can agree. This is called a ’family-based arrangement’.

There are no charges for making a family-based arrangement. Parents arrange everything themselves and no-one else has to be involved. It is flexible, and can be changed if either of your circumstances change.

You agree how much the financial contributions should be and when they should be made.

For example, you could both agree that the parent who does not have main day-to-day care of the child will pay:

  • a proportion of their income directly to the other parent
  • for things like school clothes, trips or clubs
  • a regular set amount directly to the other parent
  • a larger lump sum at different stages in the child’s life

If you agree and want a formal arrangement

There is no official paperwork for a family-based arrangement, and they are not legally binding, but you can write down your agreement in case of future disagreement.

You can ask a court to formalise your agreement using a consent order (in England and Wales), or a minute of agreement (in Scotland). These can be enforced through the courts if either parent does not do what has been agreed.
