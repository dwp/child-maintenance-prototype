Child Maintenance options

Arranging child maintenance yourself

You can arrange child maintenance between yourselves, if you can agree. This is called a ’family-based arrangement’.

You agree how much the financial contributions should be and when they should be made.

You can ask a court to formalise your agreement using a consent order (in England and Wales), or a minute of agreement (in Scotland). These can be enforced through the courts if either parent does not do what has been agreed.

If you both agree to regular payments, you can get an estimate of how much payments should be.

Get started with arranging child maintenance yourself.

Pros

  • quick and easy
  • flexible
  • maintains communication
  • no fees

Cons

  • not legally binding

Using the Child Maintenance Service

If you and your ex-partner can’t agree child maintenance payments between you, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service (CMS).

It will work out how much child maintenance should be paid and it can arrange for the money to be paid, if you want it to. There is a £20 fee for using the CMS, unless you are under 19, a victim of domestic abuse or in Northern Ireland.

There are 2 types of arrangement you can apply to make through the Child Maintenance Service:

  • Direct Pay
  • Collect and Pay

Direct Pay is for when parents cannot agree, or have tried a family-based arrangement and it has stopped working.

Collect and Pay is for when the paying parent is unlikely to pay or has failed to pay.

For Collect and Pay you must also pay a fee each time you make or receive a payment:

  • paying parents will pay a 20% fee on top of their regular child maintenance payment
  • receiving parents will have a 4% fee deducted from their regular child maintenance payment

Get started with the Child Maintenance Service.

Pros

  • payments worked out for you
  • no contact between parents required
  • no responsibility for making sure the paying parent pays child maintenance.

Cons

  • £20 application fee
  • payment and enforcement fees
  • might increase any bad feeling from your ex-partner.
  • You don’t have any say in what type of enforcement action the CMS takes or when it takes it.
