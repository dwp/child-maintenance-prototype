You are not eligible to apply to the Child Maintenance Service

The Child Maintenance Service can only enforce child maintenance arrangements where both parents and the children live in the UK.

If one of the parents lives abroad, you may need a court order for maintenance, and to apply for it to be enforced in the other country.

The UK has international agreements with more than 100 countries for child maintenance. These arrangements are called Reciprocal Enforcement of Maintenance Orders (REMO).

If a REMO is in place, it means that:

  • if a receiving parent and child live abroad, they can apply for a foreign child maintenance order which will be enforceable against the paying parent who lives in the UK
  • if the receiving parent and child live in the UK, they can seek an order to register and enforce the recovery of a child maintenance order from the paying parent who lives abroad

Find out more about arranging child maintenance if one parent lives abroad.
