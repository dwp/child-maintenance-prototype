You might also want to review your amount if your circumstances change – for example if your or the other parent changes their job.

How and when to make payments

There are several ways to pay child maintenance. The most suitable will depend on your circumstances.

If you’ve agreed on fixed regular payments, you could set up a standing order. This is where money is sent directly from one person’s bank or building society account to another person’s account.

A standing order helps both parents to plan and manage their finances, and keep a record of payments made.

How often you make payments is up to you to agree. For example it could be weekly, fortnightly or monthly.
