Calculate child maintenance

Use this calculator to work out child maintenance for your circumstances.

The calculator uses the same basic rules as the Child Maintenance Service – a government scheme for parents who cannot make their own child maintenance arrangements.

You can use the calculation as a starting point for making your own arrangement.

The receiving parent has main day-to-day care of the children. The paying parent does not have main day-to-day care.

You’ll need to know:

  • whether the paying parent gets any benefits or State Pension
  • the paying parent’s total income before tax and National Insurance, but after pension contributions
  • if any other children live with the paying parent

If you need more than one child maintenance arrangement

The Child Maintenance Service uses rules to make sure payments are split fairly between children.

Where a paying parent needs more than one arrangement, calculate the amount for the total number of children, then divide between them.

Where a receiving parent needs arrangements with more than one paying parent, a new calculation should be done for each paying parent.
