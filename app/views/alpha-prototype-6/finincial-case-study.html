Clare and Mark's finiancial arrangement

Claire and Mark recently separated after 10 years of marriage. They have 2 children, Oliver and Becky.

Claire works full time, while Mark works part-time while the children are at school. Ian and Becky both stay with Claire on Saturday nights, although Claire hopes she will be able to have them stay with her Friday evening to Sunday afternoon when she finds a new home.

The end of the relationship was quite civil, but conversations about finances have always been difficult and caused arguments. They both want to keep things friendly. Mark is keen to make an arrangement with Claire for her to pay maintenance to support their children as he has them most of the time.

Mark knows how much he spends on the children each week, on food, clothes, childcare on 2 days when he is at work, and music lessons for Ian. He suggested splitting these costs 50/50 with Claire. Claire felt that this was a lot of money, and didn’t think she should have to pay so much when she hardly got to see the children.

Claire used the child maintenance calculator to work out how much the Child Maintenance Service would calculate for her to pay when she had the children Friday to Sunday. This amount was less than what Mark was asking for, but would mean that Oliver could not continue his music lessons, which he really enjoyed.

Claire and Mark agreed that Claire would pay the amount from the Child Maintenance Calculator, plus half of Oliver’s music lessons and grade certificates. They also agreed that if Becky started music or sports lessons, they would split the cost of those as well.

Claire set up a standing order to pay the agreed amount to Mark every month, when she got paid her salary, so they both knew when the payment was due and could plan their finances around it.
