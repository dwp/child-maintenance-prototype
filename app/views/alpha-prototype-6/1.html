Making an arrangement

Child maintenance is financial support towards your child’s everyday living costs when you’ve separated from the other parent. You and the other parent can arrange child maintenance yourself if you can agree.

The cost of raising a child

One option for making an arrangement is for the paying parent to pay the parent with care a monthly amount to provide for the child. You could use the child maintenance calculator to find out how much might be a fair amount.

An arrangement doesn't have to be a fixed payment amount. It could be an agreement that the paying parent could pay:

  • for things like housing costs, uniforms, clothes, school trips, or after school clubs
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the child’s life

How to make an arrangement

Whatever the financial arrangement is, if you choose to make your own both parents will need to agree when they will be paid.

How to make payments

  • Use this arrangement tool to help you reach an agreement
  • ask a friend or family member, or find a local mediator to support you.
  • write down what you have agreed, so you have something to refer to
