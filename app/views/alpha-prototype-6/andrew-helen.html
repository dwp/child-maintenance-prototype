Andrew and Helen's finiancial arrangement

Andrew and Helen separated 2 years ago. They have 2 daughters, Hollie and Jess.

When they separated, Andrew and Helen managed to make an arrangement between themselves. They agreed that Andrew would pay a fixed amount each week, when he got paid.

Their arrangement stopped working when Andrew’s new partner had a baby and he stopped making payments as they had things to pay for in their own household.

Helen was surprised when payment suddenly stopped, and angry that the arrival of Andrew’s new baby appeared to have ended his support for Hollie and Jess.

Helen tried calling and emailing Andrew to talk about what had happened and to see if they could get their arrangement back on track, but Andrew refused to speak to her. Helen was angry, so she stopped Hollie and Jess going to see Andrew after school.

Andrew’s mum, Lilian, found out what was going on and went online looking for some advice. She found out that, as a father, Andrew was legally responsible for the costs of raising his daughters, even though he didn’t live with them.

She also found the child maintenance calculator, which makes adjustments for any other children the paying parent is responsible for.

Lilian emailed the information and the calculator to both Andrew and Helen. She asked them to put the girls first and try to resolve their differences, either by email, or they could both come to her house to talk.

Helen was surprised that Lilian was involved but appreciated her offer to help them have a conversation in her home. Andrew realised he had a legal obligation to his daughters and was keen to get an arrangement working again.

Helen and Andrew met at Lilian’s house and used the child maintenance calculator together to agree a new amount to be paid. A few weeks later, Helen also agreed to let the girls start going to Andrew’s after school again.
