You can make an arrangement yourself

There are many ways to arrange child maintenance between yourselves.

For example, you could agree to share care, split the costs of specific items, or that the non-resident parent will pay:

  • a proportion of their income to the parent with care
  • for things like housing costs, uniforms, clothes, school trips, or after school clubs
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the child’s life

If you choose to make a financial arrangement, both parents will need to agree:

  • how much financial contributions will be
  • when they will be paid
  • how payments will be made

You could also agree to review or change your arrangement in the future.

How to make payments

There are several ways to pay child maintenance. The most suitable will depend on the arrangement you have and your circumstances.

Pay by standing order

If you’ve agreed on fixed regular payments, you could set up a standing order. This is where money is sent directly from one person’s bank or building society account to another person’s account.

A standing order helps both parents to plan and manage their finances, and keep a record of payments made.

Use a money transfer service

Another option is to use a money transfer service, like PayPal or MoneyGram. This method means you don’t have to share yourbank details with the other parent.

Some of these services may charge a fee. Check with the service provider to make sure you understand if any charges will apply.

Change your payment method if your circumstances change

If you make your own arrangement, you can agree to change your payment method if your circumstances change.

For example, if the paying parent loses their job, both parents could agree to reduce the child maintenance payments until the paying parent is back in work.

Or, if the receiving parent needs to make urgent repairs to the home they live in with the children, the paying parent couldhelp with the cost by paying a lump sum, instead of the regular payments.

What to do if problems arise

Private arrangements are not legally enforceable, and you will need to work together to make sure your arrangement keeps working. A local mediator (opens in new window or tab) can help you to get things back on track if your arrangement stops working effectively.
