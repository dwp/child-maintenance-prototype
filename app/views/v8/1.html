Arrange child maintenance

Child maintenance is support to help pay for your child's everyday living costs when you are separated from the other parent.

Studies show that children get the best outcomes when their separated parents are able to work together – this includes working together to arrange child maintenance.

Making an arrangement between yourselves might not be appropriate if you think the other parent poses a threat to you or your child, or if you are a victim of domestic abuse.

Both parents are legally responsible for the costs of raising their children, even if they don’t see them.

Child maintenance is for children who are either:

  • under 16
  • under 20 and in full-time training or education (but not higher than A level or equivalent)

Where the day-to-day care of a child is shared equally between parents, neither parent is expected to pay child maintenance for them.

There are other situations when child maintenance payments stop.

Arranging child maintenance yourselves

You can arrange child maintenance yourselves, if you can agree.

Your arrangement can include whatever works for both parents.

For example, you could agree to:

  • share care of the child
  • split the costs of specific items

Or you could agree that one parent will pay:

  • some of their income to the parent the child lives with
  • for things like housing costs, uniforms, clothes, school trips, or after school clubs
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the child’s life

No one else is involved, and the arrangement can be flexible to your circumstances.

Use the Child Maintenance Service

If you cannot agree child maintenance yourselves, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service for help.

The Child Maintenance Service can work out how much child maintenance should be paid and tell both parents.

There is a £20 fee for applying to the Child Maintenance Service, unless you are:

  • under 19
  • a victim of domestic abuse
  • living in Northern Ireland

The fee does not guarantee child maintenance payments will be made.
