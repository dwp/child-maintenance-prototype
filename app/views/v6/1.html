Arrange child maintenance payments

Child maintenance is financial support to help pay for your child's everyday living costs when you are separated from the other parent.

Studies show that children get the best outcomes when their separated parents are able to work together – this includes working together to arrange child maintenance.

! Warning Both parents are legally responsible for the financial costs of raising their children, even if they don’t see them.

Arranging payments yourselves

You can arrange payments yourselves, if you can agree.

You both decide how much the financial contributions should be and when they should be made.

No one else is involved, and it can be flexible to your circumstances.

Work out child maintenance.

Using the Child Maintenance Service

If you cannot agree payments yourselves, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service for help.

The Child Maintenance Service can work out how much child maintenance should be paid and tell both parents.

There is a £20 fee for using the Child Maintenance Service, unless you are:

  • under 19
  • a victim of domestic abuse
  • living in Northern Ireland

The fee does not guarantee payments for child maintenance will be made.

If child maintenance payments are not made

The Child Maintenance Service may be able to collect the amount from the paying parent and pass it to the receiving parent – this is known as a 'Collect and Pay' arrangement. If this happens, you'll need to pay:

  • a 20% fee on top of your regular child maintenance payments, if you're the paying parent
  • a 4% fee taken off your regular child maintenance payments, if you're the recieving parent.
