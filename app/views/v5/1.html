Arrange child maintenance payments

Arranging payments yourself

You can arrange payments yourselves, if you can agree. This is called a ’family-based arrangement’.

You agree how much the financial contributions should be and when they should be made.

No one else is involved, and it can be flexible to your circumstances.

Get started with arranging payments yourself.

Using the Child Maintenance Service

If you and your ex-partner can’t agree payments between you, you can contact the Child Maintenance Service (CMS).

It will work out how much child maintenance should be paid and it can arrange for the money to be paid, if you want it to. There is a £20 fee for using the CMS, unless you are under 19, a victim of domestic abuse or in Northern Ireland.

The £20 fee does not guarantee an arrangement for child maintenance payments.

Other fees

If the paying parent is unlikely to pay or has failed to pay, a 'Collect and Pay' arrangement will be made. This means you must pay:

  • a 20% fee on top of your regular child maintenance payment, if you're the paying parent
  • a 4% fee deducted from your regular child maintenance payment, if you're the receiving parent
