If your arrangement stops working

If you have an arrangement and it is no longer working, you could ask a friend, family member, or professional family mediator (opens in new window or tab) to help you get back on track.

A mediator can help you to:

  • work together to decide what is best for your child
  • reduce conflict between you
  • build trust in each other
  • improve communications
  • make a flexible, lasting agreement for child maintenance

If you cannot make your own arrangement

If both parents cannot agree, or something else is stopping you making your own arrangement, the Child Maintenance service may be able to make and enforce an arrangement for you.
