Apply to the Child Maintenance service

Either parent can ask the Child Maintenance Service to make and enforce a new child maintenance arrangement.

What the Child Maintenance Service can do

The Child Maintenance Service can:

  • try to find the other parent if you do not know where they live, to sort out child maintenance
  • sort out disagreements about parentage (opens in new window or tab)
  • work out how much child maintenance should be paid (opens in new window or tab)
  • look at the payment amount again when changes in parents’ circumstances are reported
  • review the payment amount every year
  • take action if payments are not made (opens in new window or tab)

To apply you must:

  • live in the UK
  • be a parent or guardian of the child, or a child over 12 living in Scotland
  • pay a £20 application fee you are under 19, live in Northern Ireland, or you or your child have experienced domestic abuse

Your child must be under 20 and in full time education up to A Level or equivalent.

The Child Maintenance Service will calculate a legally enforceable amount and tell both parents when it should be paid. Parents are responsible for making sure that payments are made.

Extra charges will apply if you need the Child Maintenance Service to collect and pass on payments between you, this is called 'collect and pay' or if the service needs to enforce payments.
