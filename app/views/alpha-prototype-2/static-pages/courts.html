Courts

You can arrange child maintenance using a consent order (in England and Wales), or a minute of agreement (in Scotland). This makes the arrangement legally binding. It can be enforced through the courts if either parent does not do what has been agreed.

You cannot apply to the Child Maintenance Service for a statutory child maintenance arrangement within 12 months of setting up a consent order or a minute of agreement. If you have a consent order or minute of agreement made before April 2003, you cannot set up a statutory arrangement.
