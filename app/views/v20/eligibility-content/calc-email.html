Next steps

You can now agree how you will share the costs of raising your children. Use this calculation as a starting point.

Your arrangement can include whatever works for both parents.

For example you could agree to:

  • share care of the children
  • split the cost of specific items

Or you could agree that one parent will pay:

  • some of their income to the parent the children live with
  • for things like housing costs, uniform, clothes, school trips or after school clubs

No one else is involved, and the arrangement can be flexible to your circumstances.

Some parents find it useful to write down their arrangement (opens in a new window or tab) so they have something to refer back to.

How and when to make payments

If you agree to a financial arrangement there are several ways to pay child maintenance. The most suitable will depend on your circumstances.

If you have agreed on fixed regular payments, you could set up a standing order. This is where money is sent directly from one person’s bank or building society account to another person’s account.

A standing order can help both parents to plan and manage their finances, and keep a record of payments made.

If you have agreed to more flexible payments, these could be made by bank transfer, cheque, or pre-paid credit card.
