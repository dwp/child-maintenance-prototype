Get help agreeing

Mediation

An independent mediator can help you and your child’s other parent to agree an arrangement for child maintenance that works for both of you.

Mediation is not relationship counselling. It can help you to agree how you will both support your children.

There will be a charge for using a mediator. You might be able to get help with fees if you are eligible for Legal Aid. You can ask a court to make the arrangement you agree in mediation legally binding with a consent order.

Courts

You can arrange child maintenance using a consent order (in England and Wales), or a minute of agreement (in Scotland). This makes the arrangement legally binding. It can be enforced through the courts if either parent does not do what has been agreed.

Other ways to make child arrangements

You can get information about how to prepare to make child arrangements, and how to reach an agreement, from the get help with child arrangements service.

If you cannot agree

The Child Maintenance Service is for when you and the other parent cannot agree a child maintenance arrangement between yourselves, or when a family-based arrangement can no longer work.

You can apply to the Child Maintenance Service for a statutory child maintenance arrangement if the consent order or minute of agreement was set up more than 12 months ago.
Eligibility

You can apply if you are in the UK and you are:

  • the parent the child lives with
  • the parent the child does not live with
  • a grandparent or other guardian of the child
  • a child over 12 living in Scotland
Fees

There is a non-refundable charge of £20 to apply to the Child Maintenance Service.

You will not have to pay this if you are:

  • a victim of domestic violence
  • under 19 years of age
  • resident in Northern Ireland
