Direct Pay

Direct Pay is for when parents cannot agree, or have tried a family-based arrangement and it has stopped working.

The Child Maintenance Service works out the payment amount, then you can make your own payment arrangement if you both agree. Both parents will be told what should be paid and when.

There is a fee of £20 to apply for Direct Pay. You will not have to pay any fees if you make a family-based arrangement.

The easiest way to pay is by standing order from the paying parent’s account into the receiving parent’s account. If you don’t want to contact each other, the Child Maintenance Service can securely pass the receiving parent's bank account details to the paying parent. If you do not want the other parent to know where you live, you can set up a 'non-geographic' bank account with a central or national sort code.

The Child Maintenance Service can enforce missed payments. You should keep a record of payments in case there are any problems in the future.
