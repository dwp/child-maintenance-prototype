Child Maintenance Service arrangements

There are 2 types of arrangement you can apply to make through the Child Maintenance Service:

  • Direct pay
  • Collect and pay

Direct Pay

Direct Pay is for when parents cannot agree an amount of maintenance, or have tried a family-based arrangement and it has stopped working.

If you choose Direct Pay the Child Maintenance Service will work out the payment amount, then you can make your own payment arrangement if you both agree. The Child Maintenance Service will send both parents a letter explaining what should be paid and when.

There is a fee of £20 to apply for Direct Pay. You will not have to pay any fees if you make a family-based arrangement.

The easiest way to pay is by standing order from the paying parent s account into the receiving parent s account.

The Child Maintenance Service can enforce missed payments. You should keep a record of payments in case there are any problems in the future.

Either parent can choose Direct Pay, unless there is evidence that the paying parent is unlikely to pay.

Collect and pay

Collect and Pay is for when the paying parent is unlikely to pay or has failed to pay.

If you choose Collect and Pay, the Child Maintenance Service will work out the payment amount, then arrange to collect and pass on payments. They will arrange this around when the paying parent is paid their wages, pension or benefits.

There is a fee of £20 to apply for Collect and Pay.

You will also have to pay a fee each time you make or receive a payment:

  • paying parents will pay a 20% fee on top of their regular child maintenance payment
  • receiving parents will have a 4% fee deducted from their regular child maintenance payment

You cannot avoid collection fees by making payments in advance.

You will not have to pay any fees if you choose a family-based arrangement.

You will not have to pay collection fees if you choose Direct Pay, where you make your own payment arrangements.
