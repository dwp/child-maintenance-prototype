Making an arrangement

Child maintenance is financial support towards your child’s everyday living costs when you’ve separated from the other parent. You and your ex-partner can arrange child maintenance yourself if you can agree.

What to pay

One option for making an arrangement is for the paying parent to pay the parent with care a monthly amount to provide for the child. You could use the child maintenance calculator to find out how much might be a fair amount.

An arrangement doesn't have to be a fixed payment amount. It could be an agreement that the paying parent could pay:

  • for things like housing costs, uniforms, clothes, school trips, or after school clubs
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the child’s life

If you choose to make your own financial arrangement, both parents will need to agree:

  • how much will be paid and what is being paid for
  • when they will be paid
  • how payments will be made
  • You could also agree to review or change your arrangement in the future.

If you need help agreeing your arrangement

  • Use this arrangement tool to help you reach an agreement
  • ask a friend or family member, or find a local mediator to support you.
  • write down what you have agreed, so you have something to refer to.

Next steps

Using the tools and information provided, you can now make your own child maintenance arrangement. If you need more help you can talk to an advisor.

Making an arrangement between yourselves might not be appropriate if you think the other parent poses a threat to you or your child, or if you are a victim of domestic abuse. If you feel making an arrangement could make you vulnerable, speak to one of our agents about your options.

If you have tried your own arrangement and it has failed

If you have tried your own arrangement and it has failed, either parent can ask the Child Maintenance Service to make a new, formal arrangement.

The Child Maintenance Service will calculate a legally enforceable amount and tell both parents when it should be paid.

  • There is a £20 application fee (unless you are under 19, a victim of domestic abuse or live in Northern Ireland.)
  • Extra charges will apply if you need the Child Maintenance Service to collect and pass on payments between you, or if the service needs to enforce payments.
