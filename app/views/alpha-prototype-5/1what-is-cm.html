Make an arrangement

You and your child’s other parent can arrange child maintenance between yourselves, if you can agree.

What to include in an arrangement

Your arrangement can include whatever works for both parents.

You could agree to share care of your child, or school pick-up and drop-off responsibilities.

Many parents choose to make a financial arrangement. For example, the parent who does not live with the child might pay:

  • a fixed amount to the parent with care each month
  • for specific items for the child, like uniforms, school trips, or after school activities
  • for housing costs, like rent, mortgage payments, or household bills
  • larger lump sums at certain times in the child’s life

If you agree to a financial arrangement, you will also need to agree when payments will be made, and if you will review your arrangement in the future.

You can use the child maintenance calculator to get an estimate of how much the Child Maintenance Service would calculate as a fair amount to pay.

If you need help to make an arrangement

There are many tools available to help you to make an arrangement yourselves. You could:

  • Use this arrangement tool to help you reach an agreement
  • ask a friend or family member, or find a local mediator to support you.
  • write down what you have agreed, so you have something to refer to

Case studies

This collection of child maintenance case studies show how other people have made their own child maintenance arrangements.

If you are vulnerable
Making an arrangement between yourselves might not be appropriate if you think the other parent poses a threat to you or your child, or if you are a victim of domestic abuse. If you feel making an arrangement could make you vulnerable, speak to one of our agents about your options.
If your arrangement is no longer working

If you have an arrangement and it it no longer working, there are tools and guides on getting help and mediation. If you are unable to make an arrangement yourself can apply to the Child Maintenance service. There is a £20 fee to apply
